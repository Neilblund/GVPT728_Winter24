Course description

This course will introduce you to advanced topics in modeling and inference. Over the next three weeks, we will develop the tools to identify viable research questions, formulate hypotheses, and answer the question of what causes a social-science outcome of interest. At the conclusion of the course you will write and present a replication and extension or novel analysis that applies the skills you learned.

Required Materials

All required materials will be made available through the course website.

While you do not need to purchase anything for this course, I will occasionally recommend chapters from the following texts. These are not required, but I’ve found them useful enough as reference texts that they’re worth buying if you can find them for a reasonable price.

  • Gujarati D. N. & Porter D. C. (2009). Basic econometrics (5th ed.). McGraw-Hill. (worldcat)

  • Fox J. (2016). Applied regression analysis and generalized linear models (3.). Sage. (worldcat)

  • Achen C. H. (1982). Interpreting and using regression. Sage Publications. (worldcat)

None of these are required to do well in this course and all three should be freely available through the UMD library or as used books. 

Expectations

This course will be entirely online. To the greatest extent possible, you should try to treat this like a real classroom. That means at a minimum you should have your camera on during class time and be awake and paying attention. If this is an issue for you, please let me know. 

Major Assignments

All assignments will be submitted and graded through ELMS unless otherwise noted.

Assignments/Quizzes (30%)

The homework assignments and quizzes will be short analyses and coding tasks that are intended to enhance your understanding of the course material. You will submit these through ELMS as a compiled and annotated markdown file. For the homework assignments, you are encouraged to collaborate with your peers, but quizzes will be completed independently.

Discussion lead/response paper (25%)

Students will select two readings from the list of “Discussion Readings” and write a 1-2 page summary of each. Be prepared to briefly summarize the readings in the following class period.

Final project (35%)

Each student will select a research topic and a data set and analyze it using the skills you’ve learned in the course. You will present your findings on the final day of class, and submit a brief written memo detailing your results.

The final project grade will consist of three components:

  • Presentation (35%)

  • Memo (35%)

  • Replication code and data (30%)

Participation & Engagement (10%)

Attendance is a necessary, but not sufficient, condition for full credit here. Full participation means asking or answering questions, contributing to in-class discussions and exercises, offering constructive feedback on group projects etc.

(Any guided practice or group assignments we complete in class will also be counted towards this component of your grade)
